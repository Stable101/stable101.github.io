Tether USDT
Est. 2014 · USD-pegged stablecoin

📖 History

Tether (USDT) was launched in July 2014 by Brock Pierce, Reeve Collins, and Craig Sellars under the name Realcoin, later rebranded to Tether. It was the first fiat-backed stablecoin ever deployed, initially built on Bitcoin's Omni Layer protocol. Tether Limited, the issuing company, is closely affiliated with the Bitfinex exchange — both are owned by iFinex Inc.

USDT was controversial early on: in 2019 the New York Attorney General investigated Bitfinex for allegedly using Tether reserves to cover an $850 million loss. In 2021 Tether settled with the NYAG and the CFTC for $41 million without admitting wrongdoing, agreeing to publish quarterly reserve attestations.

Despite controversy, Tether grew into the dominant stablecoin, expanding across Ethereum, Tron, Solana, TON, and many other chains.

⚡ Main Use Cases

  • Trading & hedging — park value without leaving crypto during market volatility
  • Cross-border payments — instant, low-cost USD transfers globally, especially in emerging markets
  • Cross-chain transfers via LayerZero — USDT can be bridged across 30+ chains using LayerZero's OFT (Omnichain Fungible Token) standard, enabling seamless multi-chain liquidity
  • DeFi collateral & liquidity — largest stablecoin pool on Curve, Uniswap, and Aave
  • Remittances — popular in countries like Argentina, Turkey, and Nigeria as a USD substitute

Circle USDC
Est. 2018 · Regulated USD stablecoin

📖 History

USD Coin (USDC) was launched in September 2018 as a joint venture between Circle and Coinbase through the Centre Consortium. Unlike Tether, USDC was positioned from day one as a compliant, regulated stablecoin with monthly third-party attestations by Grant Thornton (later Deloitte).

In 2023, Centre was dissolved and Circle took full ownership of USDC. That same year, USDC experienced a brief depeg to ~$0.87 after Silicon Valley Bank (one of Circle's reserve custodians) collapsed — it recovered within days once the FDIC guaranteed deposits.

Circle filed for a US IPO in 2024 and has been working closely with regulators to position USDC as the compliant choice for institutions. USDC is natively issued on Base, Ethereum, Solana, Arbitrum, Avalanche, and many other chains.

⚡ Main Use Cases

  • Institutional payments — preferred stablecoin for banks, fintechs, and corporates due to regulatory clarity
  • Cross-chain with CCTP — Circle's Cross-Chain Transfer Protocol (CCTP) enables native USDC burns and mints across chains (no bridging risk), currently live on Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Solana, and more
  • Base ecosystem — USDC is the primary stablecoin on Base (Coinbase's L2), deeply integrated with its DeFi ecosystem
  • Programmable payments — used by Coinbase Commerce and Web3 payment platforms for e-commerce
  • DeFi & lending — major asset on Compound, Aave, and Morpho with deep liquidity

Sky DAI
Est. 2017 · Decentralised stablecoin (ex-MakerDAO)

📖 History

DAI was created by MakerDAO, founded by Rune Christensen, and launched in December 2017. It was the first decentralised stablecoin — instead of being backed 1:1 by dollars in a bank, DAI is generated by locking collateral (originally only ETH) into smart contract Vaults on Ethereum.

The system is governed by MKR token holders. In 2020, Multi-Collateral DAI (MCD) launched, allowing ETH, WBTC, and eventually even USDC as collateral. In 2023 MakerDAO launched the Endgame plan, rebranding to Sky in 2024 — introducing USDS (the successor to DAI) and SKY (the successor to MKR), while keeping DAI fully functional.

DAI remains the world's largest decentralised stablecoin and a cornerstone of DeFi.

Total Supply
~$5.4B
FDV
~$5.4B

⚡ Main Use Cases

  • Decentralised borrowing — users lock ETH/WBTC/etc. in Sky Vaults to mint DAI without a centralised issuer
  • DeFi building block — deeply integrated across Uniswap, Curve, Aave, and nearly every DeFi protocol as a trustless USD reference
  • DAI Savings Rate (DSR) — holders can earn yield by depositing DAI into the Sky protocol's savings module
  • Censorship resistance — unlike USDC/USDT, DAI has no blacklist function; ideal for protocols needing permissionless money
  • Cross-chain DeFi — bridged to Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, and Polygon; used as stable collateral in L2 ecosystems
